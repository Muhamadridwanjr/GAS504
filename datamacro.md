## What this all means for markets right now

The global macro landscape on March 6, 2026 is defined by a **classic stagflation scenario**: inflation pressures are resurging (ISM Prices Paid at 70.5, oil above $85, Eurozone CPI surprising up) while growth is deteriorating (US NFP at -92K, Eurozone GDP revised down, China targeting its lowest growth in decades). Central banks — the Fed, ECB, BOE, and BOJ — are all in holding patterns, unable to cut rates due to inflation or hike due to growth fears.

The **US-Iran conflict** is the dominant near-term driver across all asset classes. Gold benefits from safe-haven flows and inflation hedging. Oil is driven purely by supply disruption.
